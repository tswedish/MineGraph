## Analysis

### 1. focused (beam=80, depth=12, bias=0.8) is the clear winner
- 676 admits/worker — **1.28x** better than focused-wide, **1.36x** better than focused-deep
- **1.69x** better than standard
- The bias=0.8 (top-heavy sampling) continues to be the dominant factor

### 2. All three focused variants beat standard
- focused: 676/wk, focused-wide: 530/wk, focused-deep: 497/wk, standard: 400/wk
- This confirms that **bias=0.8 matters more than beam/depth tuning**
- Among the focused variants, the original (beam=80, depth=12) is best

### 3. The leaderboard is NOT saturating yet
- 98.1% admission rate after 7 hours — nearly everything submitted still gets in
- 1,034 admits/hr sustained — no decay visible
- With cap=2000 and 7,236 total admissions, entries are being replaced ~3.6x on average
- The threshold is rising but there's still room

### 4. Discovery volume vs admission efficiency
- narrow-deep finds the most graphs per round (19,450) but converts worst (113 admits/wk)
- focused finds moderate graphs per round (10,250) but converts best (676 admits/wk)
- **Quality of seeds matters more than volume of search**

### 5. Explorer (bias=0.2) underperforms all focused variants
- 347 admits/wk vs 676 for focused
- Uniform sampling explores diverse seeds but most are low quality
- Concentrated effort on the best seeds (bias=0.8) wins decisively

## Key Takeaways

1. **Optimal config for production fleet:** beam=80, depth=12, bias=0.8 (focused)
2. **The leaderboard still has headroom** — no need to change capacity yet
3. **Seed quality >> search volume** — top-heavy sampling is the most important knob
4. **Next improvements should come from algorithmic changes, not hyperparameter tuning**
   - The hyperparameter space is explored; focused is the clear winner
   - Further gains require: focused edge flipping, circulant seeds, tabu lists,
     or other techniques from docs/LITERATURE_AND_IDEAS.md
