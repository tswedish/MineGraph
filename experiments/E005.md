## Analysis

### Admission Rate

96.5% admission rate sustained over 26.6 hours against a 2000-slot leaderboard
means the board is NOT saturated. Almost everything submitted gets admitted,
indicating there is significant room for better graphs. The quality threshold
is still low relative to what the search produces.

### Worker Variance

Despite identical configs, worker admits range from 567 (W14) to 1,562 (W2) --
a 2.75x spread. Rounds are nearly identical (27,598-27,921), so the difference
is entirely in what gets admitted. Since all workers use `--init leaderboard`,
this variance comes from seed sampling luck -- workers that happen to start in
different graph basins discover more diverse graphs.

### Comparison to E004

E004 ran 7 hours with 6 hyperparameter profiles. The best profile (focused:
beam=80, depth=12, bias=0.8) achieved ~1,034 admits/hr against a fresh 2000-slot
board. E005 shows 539 admits/hr over a longer duration, which is expected --
admission rate naturally declines as the leaderboard fills with better graphs.

### Key Takeaway

Further gains require algorithmic changes, not hyperparameter tuning. The 96.5%
admission rate means we're not compute-bound -- we're exploration-bound. The
search keeps finding graphs that get admitted, but isn't pushing toward
harder-to-find structural regions.
