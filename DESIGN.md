Extremal is the project name, extremal for CLI/crates/code contexts.
Here's a quick design brief:
Core concept: Mathematical mining. The intersection of deep combinatorial search and competitive discovery — prospectors digging for rare mathematical objects.
Tone: Serious but not academic. Think competitive programming meets generative art. The math is real, the competition is real, the gems are beautiful.
Visual direction: The gem visualizations are your hero asset. Lean into the adjacency-matrix-as-crystal identity. Dark backgrounds, bright deterministic gem renders, monospace type for data, a clean sans-serif for headings. Color palette pulled from the gem renders themselves — let the math generate the brand palette.
Logo: The word "Extremal" in a geometric sans-serif, potentially with one letter replaced or augmented by a small gem/graph motif. Keep it simple enough to work as a favicon.
Typography: Something like Inter or Space Grotesk for headings, JetBrains Mono for data/code. Two fonts max.
Key pages: Landing with a hero gem animation, live leaderboard, "What is Ramsey theory?" explainer, worker setup guide.
Tagline candidates: "Mine the extremes." / "Competitive graph search." / "Find the rarest graphs."
